# Medieval Europe Card Image Prompts

Self-contained front-card prompts for batch image generation. Each card block below can be copied directly into an image tool without relying on a shared base prompt.

## Major Arcana

### The Fool

```text
Card title in plaque: THE FOOL
Required symbols (MUST include): young European traveler at a cliff edge; one foot about to step into open air; small alert dog at the traveler's feet; white rose in hand; light bundle tied to a staff; bright rising sun; distant snow mountains; open sky expressing 0 as origin, openness, and undefined possibility.
Secondary symbols / compositional cues (SHOULD include): Keep the traveler, dog, and cliff edge as the immediate read; let the rising sun and distant mountains open the upper half of the image; preserve a feeling of air, altitude, and forward motion.
Meaning to express: trust entering new experience, freedom without carelessness, necessary risk, and the beginning of the soul's journey.
Historical visual style: Late medieval European tarot illustration, Gothic manuscript illumination, early Renaissance tempera painting on vellum, 15th-century Italian/French tarot atmosphere, restrained Visconti-Sforza and Marseille tarot heritage, original composition.
Costume and setting rules: Use European medieval or early Renaissance clothing and architecture only. Prefer wool cloaks, linen tunics, leather boots, pilgrim robes, clerical vestments, knightly armor, stone roads, chapels, monasteries, castles, stained glass, heraldic ornament, carved wood, iron, vellum, and parchment.
Composition: Vertical 1:1.7 tarot card front, full-bleed illustration, slim parchment border, and a bottom title plaque or cartouche containing the exact English card title. Keep the main symbols dominant and legible before decorative detail.
Text rule: The exact English card title must be readable in the bottom plaque and must be the only text in the image. Do not render Chinese text, extra labels, paragraphs, numerals, subtitles, artist signatures, watermarks, or logos.
Strict anti-style-drift: No Chinese, Japanese, Korean, or East Asian historical style. No hanfu, kimono, qipao, wuxia, xianxia, East Asian hair ornaments, ink-wash landscape, Chinese mountain painting, palace roofs, pavilions, calligraphy scrolls, lotus as default ornament, porcelain patterns, yin-yang, Taoist talisman, origami, paper cranes, anime style, or modern fantasy trading-card effects. AetherTarot mood should remain warm editorial, contemplative, trustworthy, reflective rather than predictive, with tactile parchment surface and midnight ceremony accents, not neon and not a fantasy game.
Card-specific avoid / substitution bans: Do not replace the dog with another animal, do not remove the lifted step, and do not turn the scene into a comic prank or carnival image.
```
